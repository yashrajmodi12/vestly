Investing strategy
Long-term growth — buy and hold iPicks you buy once and mostly leave alone for years. Designed to ride out short-term dips rather than react to them — historically the lowest-effort, most evidence-backed approach for long time horizons.
Active / faster moving growth iMore concentrated bets on specific companies or trends with higher growth potential — but also bigger swings in value. These tend to need closer attention and a higher tolerance for short-term losses.
Income iPrioritizes regular cash payouts (dividends) over fast price growth. Good fit if generating ongoing income matters more to you than maximizing long-term growth.
Sector bets iConcentrated in one part of the economy (like technology or global markets) rather than spread evenly across everything. Higher potential reward if that sector does well, but less diversified if it doesn't.
